
# Confusing Words & Homophones Guide

## Essential Word Pairs for Competitive Exams

---

## Category 1: Sound-Alike Words (Homophones)

### A

**accept** vs **except**

- **accept** – receive willingly, agree to
    - _"I accept your offer."_
- **except** – excluding, other than
    - _"Everyone came except John."_

**advice** vs **advise**

- **advice** – (noun) recommendation, guidance
    - _"She gave me good advice."_
- **advise** – (verb) to recommend, suggest
    - _"I advise you to study hard."_

**affect** vs **effect**

- **affect** – (verb) to influence, impact
    - _"The weather affects my mood."_
- **effect** – (noun) result, consequence; (verb) to bring about
    - _"The effect was immediate."_
    - _"We will effect the changes tomorrow."_

**aisle** vs **isle**

- **aisle** – passage between seats
    - _"Walk down the aisle."_
- **isle** – island
    - _"A tropical isle."_

**aloud** vs **allowed**

- **aloud** – audibly, out loud
    - _"Read aloud in class."_
- **allowed** – permitted
    - _"You are allowed to leave."_

**altar** vs **alter**

- **altar** – raised structure for worship
    - _"The bride stood at the altar."_
- **alter** – to change, modify
    - _"Alter your plans."_

### B

**bare** vs **bear**

- **bare** – naked, uncovered, empty
    - _"Bare feet on the sand."_
- **bear** – carry, endure; large animal
    - _"Bear the burden."_
    - _"A grizzly bear."_

**base** vs **bass**

- **base** – foundation, bottom; morally low
    - _"Base of the mountain."_
    - _"A base motive."_
- **bass** – low-pitched sound/voice; type of fish
    - _"Bass guitar."_

**board** vs **bored**

- **board** – flat piece of wood; group of directors; get on
    - _"Wooden board."_
    - _"Board of directors."_
    - _"Board the plane."_
- **bored** – uninterested, tired
    - _"I'm bored with this game."_

**brake** vs **break**

- **brake** – stopping device
    - _"Apply the brakes."_
- **break** – fracture, interrupt, pause
    - _"Break the glass."_
    - _"Take a break."_

**bread** vs **bred**

- **bread** – baked food
    - _"Slice of bread."_
- **bred** – past tense of breed; raised
    - _"Well-bred children."_

**buy** vs **by** vs **bye**

- **buy** – purchase
    - _"Buy groceries."_
- **by** – near, through, before
    - _"Stand by me."_
    - _"By tomorrow."_
- **bye** – goodbye; automatic advancement
    - _"Say bye."_
    - _"Got a bye in the tournament."_

    - _"Prison cell."_
    - _"Cell phone."_
- **sell** – exchange for money
    - _"Sell the car."_

**cent** vs **scent** vs **sent**

- **cent** – monetary unit (1/100 of dollar)
    - _"Costs fifty cents."_
- **scent** – smell, fragrance
    - _"Sweet scent of roses."_
- **sent** – past tense of send
    - _"I sent a letter."_

**cereal** vs **serial**

- **cereal** – breakfast grain food
    - _"Bowl of cereal."_
- **serial** – in series/sequence; story in parts
    - _"Serial number."_
    - _"TV serial."_

**coarse** vs **course**

- **coarse** – rough in texture; crude
    - _"Coarse sandpaper."_
    - _"Coarse language."_
- **course** – path, direction; class; part of meal
    - _"Course of action."_
    - _"Math course."_
    - _"Main course."_

**complement** vs **compliment**

- **complement** – something that completes or enhances
    - _"Wine complements the meal."_
- **compliment** – expression of praise
    - _"She paid me a compliment."_

### D

    - _"My dear friend."_
    - _"Too dear to buy."_
- **deer** – animal
    - _"A herd of deer."_

**desert** vs **dessert**

- **desert** – (DEZ-urt) dry land; (dez-URT) abandon
    - _"Sahara desert."_
    - _"Desert the sinking ship."_
- **dessert** – sweet course after meal
    - _"Ice cream for dessert."_
    - **Memory tip:** Dessert has two 's' because you want more!

**device** vs **devise**

- **device** – (noun) tool, gadget
    - _"Electronic device."_
- **devise** – (verb) to invent, plan
    - _"Devise a strategy."_

**die** vs **dye**

- **die** – cease to live; singular of dice
    - _"Plants die without water."_
    - _"Roll the die."_
- **dye** – coloring substance; to color
    - _"Hair dye."_
    - _"Dye the fabric blue."_

- **emigrate** – leave one's country to live elsewhere
    - _"Emigrate from India."_

    - _"Immigrate to America."_

**flour** vs **flower**

    - _"Rose flower."_

- **four** – number 4
    - _"Four people."_
- **fore** – front part; golf warning
    - _"To the fore."_

- **knight** – medieval warrior; chess piece

- **know** – be aware of, have knowledge

    - _"No, I disagree."_
    - _"No money left."_

### L

**lead** vs **led**

- **lead** – (LEED) to guide; be in front; (LED) heavy metal
    - _"Lead the team."_
    - _"Lead pipes."_
- **led** – past tense of lead (LEED)
    - _"She led the group."_

**lie** vs **lay**

- **lie** – recline; tell untruth (lie-lay-lain OR lie-lied-lied)
    - _"I lie down to rest."_ (present)
    - _"I lay down yesterday."_ (past - reclining)
    - _"He lied to me."_ (past - untruth)
- **lay** – put/place something (lay-laid-laid)
    - _"Lay the book on the table."_
    - _"She laid the baby in the crib."_

**loan** vs **lone**

- **loan** – borrowed money/item
    - _"Bank loan."_
- **lone** – solitary, single
    - _"Lone survivor."_

**loose** vs **lose**

- **loose** – not tight, free
    - _"Loose clothing."_
    - _"The dog broke loose."_
- **lose** – be deprived of; fail to win
    - _"Lose weight."_
    - _"Lose the game."_

**losing** vs **loosing**

- **losing** – failing to win; being deprived of
    - _"Our team is losing."_
- **loosing** – releasing, setting free
    - _"Loosing an arrow."_ (archaic/formal)

## Category 4: Contractions and Possessives

**Contraction Rule:** If you can replace it with "__ is" or "__ has," use the apostrophe. **Possessive Rule:** No apostrophe for possessive pronouns (its, whose, yours, etc.)

### Common Pairs

- **its** (possessive) vs **it's** (it is/has)
- **your** (possessive) vs **you're** (you are)
- **their** (possessive) vs **they're** (they are) vs **there** (location)
- **whose** (possessive) vs **who's** (who is/has)
- **won't** (will not) vs **wont** (habit/custom - rare)
