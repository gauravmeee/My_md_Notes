
## Useful Word Groups 

### 1. Animal Sounds (Onomatopoeia)

**Common Animals**
- **bark** – dog
- **meow/mew** – cat
- **roar** – lion, tiger
- ==**growl** – bear, dog== (aggressive)
- ==**howl** – wolf, dog== (prolonged)
- **neigh/whinny** – horse
- **bray** – donkey
- **moo** – cow
- ==**bleat** – sheep, goat==
- **oink/grunt** – pig
- **bellow** – bull, elephant

**Birds**
- **chirp/tweet** – small birds
- ==**crow** – rooster==
- ==**caw** – crow==
- ==**hoot** – owl==
- **coo** – dove, pigeon
- **quack** – duck
- **honk** – goose
- **cluck** – hen
- **squawk** – parrot (loud, harsh)
- **warble** – songbird (melodious)

**Insects & Reptiles**
- **buzz** – bee, fly
- **hum** – bee (softer)
- **chirp** – cricket
- ==**hiss** – snake==
- ==**croak** – frog==

**Other Sounds**
- **patter** – quick light tapping (rain, feet)
- **clatter** – loud rattling noise (dishes, hooves)
- **rustle** – soft crackling sound (leaves, paper)
- **splash** – water sound
- **drip** – water falling in drops
- **rumble** – low rolling sound (thunder, stomach)
- **crackle** – series of small sharp sounds (fire)
- **sizzle** – hissing sound (frying)
- **thud** – dull heavy sound (falling object)
- **bang/crash** – loud explosive sound
- **whistle** – high-pitched sound (wind, person)
- **murmur** – soft indistinct sound (crowd, stream)

### 6. Movement Verbs (How Animals Move)

**Walking/Running**
- **stride** – walk with long steps
- **march** – walk with regular steps
- **trot** – run at moderate pace (horse)
- **gallop** – run fast (horse)
- **canter** – controlled gallop
- **prance** – spring about (horse, person)
- **amble** – walk leisurely
- **waddle** – walk with short steps, swaying (duck, penguin)
- **prowl** – move stealthily (predator)
- **saunter** – walk casually
- **lumber** – move heavily and clumsily (bear, elephant)
- **scurry** – move hurriedly with short steps (mouse)
- **scamper** – run quickly and playfully
- **dash** – run very quickly
- **sprint** – run at full speed (short distance)

**Jumping/Leaping**
- **hop** – jump on one foot; small jump (rabbit, frog)
- **skip** – move with light bouncing steps
- **leap** – jump high or far
- **bound** – leap energetically
- **vault** – jump over using hands
- **spring** – jump suddenly

**Flying**
- **soar** – fly high without flapping (eagle)
- **glide** – fly smoothly without power
- **hover** – remain in one place in air (hummingbird)
- **flutter** – fly with quick light movements (butterfly)
- **swoop** – dive down suddenly (hawk)
- **dive** – plunge downward steeply

**Swimming**
- **wade** – walk through water
- **paddle** – swim with short strokes
- **dive** – plunge into water
- **float** – rest on water surface
- **drift** – move slowly with current

**Crawling/Slithering**
- **crawl** – move on hands and knees
- **creep** – move slowly and carefully
- **slither** – slide smoothly (snake)
- **wriggle** – twist and turn (worm)

### 7. Groups of People

**Professional/Official**
- **committee** – group making decisions
- **board** – directors/trustees
- **council** – advisory or legislative group
- **panel** – group of experts/judges
- **jury** – group deciding in court
- **delegation** – representatives
- **staff** – employees
- **crew** – workers (ship, plane, film)
- **cast** – actors in play/film
- **faculty** – teachers in institution
- **congregation** – religious gathering

**Social/Casual**
- **crowd** – large disorganized group
- **mob** – angry or unruly crowd
- **throng** – densely packed crowd
- **gathering** – people coming together
- **assembly** – formal gathering
- **audience** – people watching performance
- **spectators** – people watching event

**Family/Related**
- **family** – related people
- **clan** – extended family group
- **tribe** – social division in traditional society
- **brood** – children in family (informal)
- **offspring** – children/descendants

**Military/Organized**
- **army** – large military force
- **troop** – military unit
- **battalion** – army unit (300-1000 soldiers)
- **squad** – small military unit; sports team
- **platoon** – military subdivision
- **fleet** – group of ships/vehicles
- **convoy** – group of vehicles traveling together

**Performance/Entertainment**
- **band** – musicians
- **orchestra** – large instrumental group
- **choir** – singers
- **troupe** – performers (theater, circus)
- **ensemble** – group performing together

**Sports**
- **team** – sports players
- **squad** – sports team members
- **league** – group of competing teams

### 8. Groups of Things

**Nature**
- **grove** – group of trees
- **forest** – large area with trees
- **copse** – small group of trees
- **thicket** – dense group of bushes
- **bouquet/bunch** – flowers
- **cluster** – things close together
- **clump** – trees, grass growing together
- **constellation** – group of stars

**Objects**
- **stack** – orderly pile
- **heap** – untidy pile
- **bundle** – things tied together
- **batch** – things produced together
- **set** – matching items
- **collection** – gathered items
- **array** – impressive display/arrangement
- **assortment** – variety of things

**Information/Documents**
- **series** – connected things in order
- **sequence** – things following order
- **range** – variety within limits
- **suite** – connected rooms/software
- **archive** – collection of records
- **anthology** – collection of writings
- **compendium** – collection of information

**Food**
- **bunch** – grapes, bananas
- **bundle** – asparagus, wheat
- **loaf** – bread
- **slice** – bread, cake
- **sheaf** – grain, papers

- **pitcher** – jug for pouring
- **flask** – small bottle
- **vial** – very small bottle
- **urn** – large vase
- **cauldron** – large cooking pot
- **basin** – wide shallow container

**Quantities**
- **pinch** – small amount (salt)
- **dash** – small amount (liquid)
- **dollop** – lump of soft food
- **morsel** – small piece of food
- **sliver** – thin piece
- **chunk** – thick solid piece
- **slab** – flat thick piece
- **shred** – small strip
- **fragment** – small broken piece
