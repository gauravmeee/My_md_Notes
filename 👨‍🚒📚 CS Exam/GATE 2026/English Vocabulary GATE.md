# GATE Vocabulary Reference Guide

## Essential Words for 'Most Appropriate Words' Questions


##### **1. High-Frequency Academic Words**

**Intensity & Degree**
- ==**utterly** – completely==, absolutely
- ==**substantial** – significan==t, considerable in size or importance
- ==**inevitable** – unavoidable==, certain to happen
- **imperative** – absolutely necessary, essential
- **pristine** – unspoiled, in perfect condition

**Increase/Worsen**
- **exacerbated** – made worse, intensified (a problem)
- **deteriorate** – become progressively worse
- **culminate** – reach the highest or final point

##### Decrease/Improve
- ==**mitigated** – reduced the severity== of something
- **undiminished** – not reduced, remaining at full strength

##### Precision & Accuracy
- **ascertained** – determined with certainty, found out definitely
- **exactitude** – precision, accuracy

##### **2. Personality & Attitude**

**Negative Traits**
- **miserliness** – extreme stinginess, unwillingness to spend
- **insolence** – rudeness, disrespectful behavior
- **insouciance** – careless indifference, lack of concern
- **querulousness** – habitual complaining, tendency to whine
- **spite** – malice, desire to hurt others

**Positive Traits**
- **altruist** – selfless person who cares about others' welfare
- **intrepidity** – fearlessness, bravery
- **perseverance** – persistence despite difficulties
- **cordial** – warm and friendly
- **affably** – in a polite, friendly manner
- **amiably** – in a pleasant, good-natured way

**States of Mind**
- **contentment** – satisfaction, peaceful happiness
- **jaded** – tired, bored, cynical from overexposure
- **oblivious** – unaware, not conscious of surroundings
- **daunted** – intimidated, discouraged by difficulty
- ==**fazed** – disturbed==, unsettled, thrown off
- **baffled** – confused, puzzled
- **glum** – gloomy, dejected

##### **3. Communication & Expression**

**Criticism & Negativity**
- **disparage** – criticize unfairly, belittle
- **acerbic** – sharp and biting in speech or tone
- ==**curtly** – in a rudely brief manner==
- **bicker** – argue over petty matters
- **insinuation** – indirect suggestion, hint (usually negative)

**Praise & Flattery**
- ==**applaud** – praise==, express approval
- ==**flatter** – praise excessively== or insincerely⭐
- ==**fawn** – flatter excessively==, show servile attention
- **compliment** – praise (noun/verb)
- **felicitated** – congratulated formally

**Deception & Concealment**
- **obfuscate** – deliberately confuse or make unclear
- **belied** – failed to justify; gave a false impression of
- **betrayed** – revealed (unintentionally) or deceived
- **harbours** – shelters; holds secretly (thoughts, feelings)


##### **4. Desire & Motivation**

**Strong Desires**
- **voracious** – having a huge appetite (literal or figurative)
- **hankering** – strong desire or craving
- **ambition** – strong desire to achieve success

**Reluctance & Resistance**
- **begrudge** – resent giving or allowing something
- **indifferent** – unconcerned, showing no interest
- **timid** – shy, lacking confidence
##### **5. Action & Movement**

**Sudden Actions**
- **plunged** – fell or moved suddenly downward
- ==**abrupt** – sudden ==and unexpected
- **flutter** – flap wings rapidly; move with quick motions

**Evasion & Avoidance**
- **dodge** – evade, avoid skillfully
- **dither** – hesitate indecisively
- ==**skirting** – avoiding==, going around

**Investigation & Examination**
- ==**probed** – investigated deepl==y and thoroughly

**Destruction & Creation**
- **obliterate** – wipe out completely, destroy utterly
- **raze** – completely destroy (especially buildings)
- **fused** – joined firmly together
- **incubating** – developing gradually

##### **6. Descriptions & Characteristics**

**Physical Descriptions**
- **parched** – extremely dry
- **meandering** – winding, following an indirect path
- **convoluted** – extremely complex, twisted
- **fissure** – crack, split
- **dormant** – inactive, sleeping

**Quality & State**
- ==**mediocre** – average==, not impressive, ordinary
- **grandiose** – pompous, exaggerated, overly ambitious
- ==**utopian** – idealistic but impractical== ⭐
- **hyperbolic** – exaggerated, overstated
- **extraneous** – irrelevant, not essential
- **collateral** – secondary but related

**Character & Behavior**
- ==**resilient** – able to recover quickly== from difficulties
- **unruly** – uncontrollable, disorderly
- **errant** – straying from the correct path
- **poised** – balanced and ready; composed
##### **7. Scarcity & Abundance**

- **paucity** – scarcity, lack of something
- **preponderance** – majority, dominance in number
- **feast** – large meal; to enjoy something greatly

##### **8. Tendency & Likelihood**

- **propensity** – natural tendency or inclination
- **contingent** – dependent on certain conditions
- **inevitability** – certainty that something will occur
- **aberration** – deviation from what is normal or expected

##### **9. Financial & Economic Terms**

- **depreciated** – reduced in value over time
- **prosperity** – wealth, economic success
- **frugality** – economical use of resources, thriftiness

##### **10. Behavioral Influence**

**Encouraging**
- **incitements** – things that encourage or provoke action
- **pander** – cater to someone's desires (often base desires)

**Discouraging**
- **disincentives** – factors that discourage certain behavior
- **restraints** – limits, restrictions that hold back

##### **11. Weather & Natural Phenomena**

- ==**drizzle** – light rain==
- ==**downpour** – heavy rain==

##### **12. Commonly Confused Word Pairs**

**Awareness & Morality**
- **conscious** – aware, awake
- **conscience** – moral sense of right and wrong
- **consensus** – general agreement

**Movement & Force**
- **impel** – push forward, drive to action
- **dispel** – drive away, cause to disappear
- **propel** – force ahead, push forward with force
- **repel** – push back, drive away

**Official Actions**
- **prescribed** – officially ordered or recommended
- **proscribed** – officially banned or forbidden
- **facilitated** – made easy or possible
- **felicitated** – congratulated

**Appearance & Reality**
- **apparent** – obvious, clearly visible
- **eminent** – famous, distinguished
- **imminent** – about to happen very soon
- **prominent** – important, standing out
- **dominant** – controlling, most influential

 **Other Important Pairs**
- **vain** – excessively proud; futile
- **vein** – blood vessel
- **bear** – carry, endure
- **bare** – naked, uncovered
- **complement** – something that completes
- **compliment** – expression of praise
- **course** – path, direction
- **coarse** – rough in texture
- **quite** – fairly, rather, very
- **quiet** – silent, making little noise
- **piece** – a part of something
- **peace** – absence of conflict
- **break** – fracture, interrupt
- **brake** – stopping device
- **losing** – failing to win
- **loosing** – releasing, setting free

##### **13. Advanced Academic Vocabulary**

**Behavior & Actions**
- **reneged** – went back on a promise or commitment
- **reprise** – repeat or resume
- **tampering** – interfering wrongly, altering improperly
- **cherish** – value deeply, hold dear

**Difficult & Rare**
- **fritter** – waste gradually (time, money)
- ==**frizzle** – fry== or burn lightly
- **exhilarating** – very exciting, thrilling
- **nonchalance** – calm indifference, casual lack of concern
- ==**inertness** – inactivity==, lack of movement

**Groups & People**
- **delegations** – groups of representatives
- **plaintiff** – person who files a legal case
- **atheist** – person who does not believe in God

**Objects**
- **figurine** – small decorative statue
- **apparel** – clothing
- **ivory** – hard white material from elephant tusks
- **memento** – souvenir, keepsake

**Mental States**
- **desperation** – state of hopelessness and despair
- **fantasized** – imagined unrealistically

##### **14. Useful Word Groups**

**Sounds**
- **croak** – deep harsh sound (frog)
- **patter** – quick light tapping sound
- **hiss** – sharp sibilant sound
- **roar** – loud deep sound

**Collective Nouns**
- **heap** – pile of things
- **herd** – group of large animals
- **flock** – group of birds/sheep
- **swarm** – large group of insects

##### **15. Context-Dependent Words**

==**"Betrayed"**==
1. ==**revealed**== (unintentionally) – "His expression betrayed his nervousness"
2. **deceived** – "She felt betrayed by his lies"

**"Rational"**
- **logical** – based on reason and sound judgment

==**"Restrain"**==
- **hold back** – prevent from doing something

==**"Setbacks"**==
- **obstacles** – things that hinder progress

==**"Virtues"**==
- **good qualities** – moral excellence or positive traits
